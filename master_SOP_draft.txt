## **4. Budget & Cash Control Workflow**

* **Who:** Single Staff Member (Execution & Logging) & Business Owner (Approval & Review).
* **When:**
* **Recording:** Immediately when an expense occurs.
* **Review:** The **1st Business Day** of every month.


* **What Information:**
* **Fixed Costs:** Date paid, Provider (e.g., Internet ISP), Amount.
* **Variable Costs:** Client Name, Type (Travel/Print), Amount.
* **Buffer Status:** Total Revenue minus Total Costs = Net Cash (Amount to transfer to Savings).


* **What Format:** "Monthly Budget Tracker" (Excel Sheet or Physical Ledger Book) with three distinct sections: **Fixed**, **Variable**, and **Reserve**.
* **How to keep log for audit:**
* **Receipts:** Photo of every physical receipt (taxi, printing shop) uploaded to an "Expenses_[Month]" folder.
* **Naming:** File names must include date and amount (e.g., `2023-10-12_Taxi_5000MMK.jpg`).


* **Who certify it:** Business Owner (Must sign off on the Monthly P&L report).
* **How to follow up:**
* **Over-Budget Alert:** If Variable Costs for a specific client exceed **10% of that client's contract value**, stop spending and notify the Owner immediately.
* **Savings Transfer:** Immediately transfer the calculate "Cash Buffer" to the separate Reserve Bank Account on the 1st of the month.



### **The Process (Expense Categories)**

#### **A. Fixed Costs (The "Keep the Lights On" Fund)**

*These must be paid first, usually on the 1st-5th of the month.*

1. **Internet/Wifi:** (e.g., Fiber Bill)
2. **Phone/Data:** (Staff SIM card top-up)
3. **Transport Allowance:** (Fixed monthly stipend if applicable)
4. **Shop Utility:** (Electricity/Water)

#### **B. Variable Costs (The "Per-Client" Costs)**

*These are costs triggered only when we serve a client. tracked against that specific Client's Revenue.*

1. **Travel:** Taxi/Bus fare for on-site demos or installation.
2. **Printing:** Contracts, invoices, and marketing brochures given to the client.
3. **Support:** Any direct costs to fix a client's issue (e.g., replacing a cable).

#### **C. Savings Target (The "Cash Buffer")**

* **Rule:** **20% of Net Profit** (Revenue - Expenses) must be saved.
* **Action:** Once the monthly reconciliation is done, transfer this amount to a separate "Emergency Fund" account. This ensures the business survives slow months.

### **How to set up the Formulas (Copy & Paste)**

If you are building this in Excel, here are the simple formulas to use in **Tab 3**:

1. **Total Fixed Costs:** `=SUM('Tab 1'!D:D)` (Assuming Amount is in Column D)
2. **Total Variable Costs:** `=SUM('Tab 2'!E:E)` (Assuming Amount is in Column E)
3. **Savings Target (20%):** `=IF(Net_Profit > 0, Net_Profit * 0.20, 0)`
* *Note:* The `IF` statement prevents the sheet from asking you to save money if you made a loss (negative profit).

* **What Format:** "Monthly Business Health Dashboard" (One-page summary sheet).
* **How to keep log for audit:** Save the dashboard as PDF in a folder named `Year_M&E_Reports`. Compare the current month against the previous month (Month-over-Month growth).
* **Who certify it:** Business Owner.
* **How to follow up:**
* **If Green (On Target):** Approve the budget for next month.
* **If Red (Warning):** Freeze non-essential spending and activate the "Early Warning Actions" (see below).
